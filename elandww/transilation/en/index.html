IELTS TRAINING PROGRAMS FOR NURSES

Kaplan International English has been working with the NHS for more than four years, providing invaluable language and integration support for both existing and newly recruited international staff.

Most recently, we have been focusing on English language training for international nurses who need to pass the International English Language Test (IELTS) at level 7.0 or above

IELTS PROGRAM

Our program consists of three main components:

  • Assessment Day to establish the English level of the nurses
  • Two-week intensive IELTS program (tailored to the needs of each cohort)
  • Weekly follow up sessions to get nurses to the required level

All books, materials and online access are included as part of the course.

Why choose Kaplan?

  • In 2018 we celebrate 80 years of experience in helping international students achieve their goals.
  • Our courses are designed around teaching students specific test-taking strategies combined with academic English skills to create well-rounded strong test takers.
  • A large proportion of our IELTS teachers are IELTS examiners or have contributed to the writing of academic materials in relation to IELTS.
  • We have an excellent track record in our students getting through their IELTS.

CASE STUDY

A Manchester based NHS Trust

Challenge:

Gaps in the workforce meant that this NHS Trust needed to recruit international nurses; however these highly skilled individuals did not have their IELTS 7.0. The Trust had utilised local English Language teachers but with no qualified ‘IELTS specialists’ they were seeing very little progress and many international nurses were failing achieve the high score required.

Outcomes:

All nurses who took the IELTS exam following the three-stop process passed their IELTS at the required level. Kaplan is now working on its third cohort of nurses with this trust.

Kaplan solution:

Kaplan carried out a thorough needs analysis and implemented a 3-step process

  • Assessment day: on-site session held by one of our IELTS examiners which included a thorough needs analysis followed by a knowledge, writing, reading, speaking and listening test for the entire cohort.
  • Two weeks intensive: using the results from the assessment day, Kaplan recommended a two-week intensive English language program.
  • Follow up sessions: weekly follow-up sessions were then carried out on the hospital premises for the next X weeks

Throughout the 3-step process, Kaplan reported in to the Director of Nursing on the progress of each nurse.
